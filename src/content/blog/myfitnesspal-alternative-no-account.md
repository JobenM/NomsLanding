## Why People Look for a MyFitnessPal Alternative

MyFitnessPal is one of the most established calorie trackers in the world, with a huge food database and a long track record. For a lot of people it does the job well.

But it isn't for everyone. The common reasons people start hunting for an alternative tend to come down to a few things:

- **You have to create an account before you can log anything.** Email, password, profile, goals — all before your first meal.
- **Some features moved behind a paywall.** In 2022, MyFitnessPal moved its barcode scanner to its paid Premium tier, a change that frustrated many long-time free users.
- **Logging can feel like database admin.** Its database is enormous but crowd-sourced, so you often scroll past duplicate and inconsistent entries to find the "right" one.
- **Some long-time users feel it's grown heavier.** If you've found recent versions busier than the simple food log you first signed up for, plenty of users have said the same.

None of that makes MyFitnessPal a bad app. It just means a faster, lighter, more private approach appeals to a lot of people. That's the gap NomsAI was built for.

## What to Look for in an Alternative

Before naming names, here's what actually matters when you're switching trackers:

**Speed of logging.** The single biggest predictor of whether you'll stick with tracking is how little effort each entry takes. Searching a database is slower than just saying what you ate.

**How much it asks of you up front.** Every field between download and your first log is a chance to give up. The best tools let you start in seconds.

**What happens to your data.** A food log is personal — it can reveal health conditions, habits, and struggles. Where it's stored, and whether it's tied to your identity, matters.

**Honest pricing.** Most apps have a free tier and a paid tier. What counts is whether the *everyday* job stays usable for free, and whether the paywall lands on nice-to-haves rather than essentials.

## How NomsAI Compares

NomsAI takes a deliberately different approach to the same problem.

**No account, ever.** There's no sign-up. Open the app and an anonymous device ID is generated locally — that's the only identifier that exists. No email, no password, no profile to fill in.

**Type or speak, instead of searching.** You write (or say) "2 eggs and toast" and NomsAI does the lookup for you — matching known and branded foods to its nutrition database and using AI to parse your words and estimate anything that isn't on file. No scrolling past five near-identical entries to find the right one.

**Eating out actually works.** Restaurant meals are where database-only tracking falls down — if the exact dish from a local or independent place isn't listed, you're left guessing or rebuilding it ingredient by ingredient. With NomsAI you just describe what you had — "chicken katsu curry and a spring roll" — and it estimates the whole plate in one go. Same for home-cooked dinners and anything that never had a barcode.

**Your data stays on your device.** Your food log is stored locally on your phone, not on our servers. The text you type is processed to estimate macros and then discarded — it's never stored or linked to you.

**Barcode scanning is free.** Scanning a packaged product doesn't require a subscription in NomsAI.

**It stays simple on purpose.** The core loop is a single input box and your day's totals. That's the product.

## Being Honest About the Trade-offs

A fair comparison cuts both ways, so here's where NomsAI asks something of you — or where MyFitnessPal may genuinely suit you better:

- **NomsAI isn't unlimited-for-free.** Everyday logging is free up to a daily allowance; unlimited logging, the recipe builder, and AI meal suggestions are part of NomsAI Pro. Barcode scanning and core logging stay free.
- **Estimates you can correct.** NomsAI pairs a nutrition database (for known and branded foods, including barcode scans) with AI that parses your input and fills in anything not on file. For a loosely described or home-cooked meal that's a close estimate rather than a lab-exact figure — so every entry stays editable to the gram before you save it.
- **No automatic cross-device sync.** Because there's no account, your log doesn't auto-appear on a new phone. NomsAI handles this with manual backup/restore (export and import a file) — a deliberate trade for keeping your data off our servers. If you switch phones constantly and want seamless sync, an account-based app may fit you better.

## Who Each App Is For

**MyFitnessPal makes sense if** you want the largest possible food database, you rely on automatic cross-device sync, and you don't mind an account and a subscription for some features.

**NomsAI makes sense if** you want to start logging in seconds with no account, you'd rather describe a meal than search for it, you value your data staying on your device, and you want barcode scanning without a paywall.

The best tracker is the one you'll actually keep using. For some people that's the full-featured, database-driven, account-based model MyFitnessPal offers. For a growing number of people, it's something lighter: no account, no database admin, fast AI logging, and a food log that never leaves their phone.

If that second description sounds like you, NomsAI is worth a try — and you can be logging your first meal about thirty seconds after you download it, no sign-up required.
